Around the turn of the 20th century,
scientists began stirring up what would ultimately become a world-changing revolution.
They deployed cleverly designed experiments and careful reasoning to reveal a realm billions of times smaller than
the dust specks they might have wiped from their equipment. There they witnessed the basic ingredients of the universe -- such as electrons and light -- behaving in deeply surprising ways. Their discoveries transformed society and birthed a new scientific field: quantum physics. Computers chips, lasers, GPS -- in short, modernity -- all rest on the bedrock of this field.
